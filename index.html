Plier Definition

End Cutting- End cutting pincers, also known as end cutters, nippers or end cutting pliers, are used to cut through materials such as wire, springs, nails, rivets and bolts. The unique design allows you cut close against the surface without digging into it. They feature much sharper jaws than carpenter's pincers and an almost flat bladed head.

The video review provides you with the top five end cutting pliers. If you want to see more the best end cutting pliers click HERE.

Channellocks- Tongue-and-groove pliers are also known as water pump pliers, adjustable pliers, slippy pliers, groove-joint pliers, arc-joint pliers, Multi-Grips, tap or pipe spanners, gland pliers and Channellocks. These pliers have a great deal of uses for their serrated jaws, which generally set 45 to 60 degrees from the handles. The adjustable lower jaw can be moved to a number of position. This movement gives an advantage against other pliers by allowing for a number of sizes to be reached without the distance in the handle growing wider. However, these particular pliers often have long handles (commonly 9.5 to 12 inches long). This increased length allows for a greater leverage when working with difficult nuts, bolts, pipes, etc.

The video review provides you with the top five channellock pliers. If you want to see more on the best channellock pliers click HERE.

Needle Nose- Needle-nose pliers are also known as pointy-nose pliers, long-nose pliers, pinch-nose pliers or snipe-nose pliers. They are designed for both cutting and holding. The pliers are used by artisans, jewellery designers, electricians, network engineers and other tradesmen to bend, re-position and snip wire. Their long pointed appearance is useful when reaching spaces where fingers can not.

The video review provides you with the top five needle nose pliers. If you want to see more on the best needle nose pliers click HERE.

Diagonal Cutting- Diagonal cutting pliers are also known as side-cutters, diagonal cutters, “dikes,” or even “diags.” Their main purpose is to cut wire. They are not tipically used to grab things. The handlesare generally insulated with a dip-type or shrink fit electrically-insulating material. This is implemented for both comfort and some protection against electric shock.
